Arsen Babajanyan

Biography

Arsen Babajanyan is Munich based Armenian composer. He was born in Yerevan, Armenia in 1986. After taking his bachelor's and masters degrees in homeland, he moved to Warsaw in 2012 and completed a six-month study with the composer Paweł Mykietyn. From 2012 to 2018 Arsen Babajanyan continued his master studies at the University of Music and Performing Arts Munich in the composition class of Prof. Jan Müller-Wieland. Afterwards he completed post-graduation in Conservatory for Music and Theatre, Graz in subject of “Composition and Music Theory" with Prof. Beat Furrer. In addition to my main occupation as a composer, I am actively engaged in a social-cultural activities. He is a member of Armenian Composers Union, Chairman of the Association of Armenian Young Composers and Interpreters since 2010, co-founder and art-director of ARTCONCEPT International Association since 2016, which realises art projects and programs all over the world. Arsen Babajanyan is author of several books, publications, literaral and critical articles.

03
Honors & Prizes

During his professional activity Arsen Babajanyan has been awarded several prizes, including 1st prize for the ensemble piece "Traerp" at the 8th pre-art Competition for young composers in Zurich in 2014, the Best Film Music Award at the Żubroffka Festival in Poland, and the DAAD Award for outstanding academic achievement in 2017. In 2015 he participated in BridgArt artists residency and presented his electronic music in scope of “Oggi” live performance. In January 2018 he received the 3rd Prize for the 4th String Quartet at the Felix Mendelssohn Bartholdy University Competition in Berlin. In 2019 he was honoured to get 6-month scholarship - residence from Minister of Culture of Bavaria in Paris at Cité Internationale des Arts. In 2017 his Piano Trio won the Best Chamber Music Piece of Year by the Composers’ Union of Armenia.

04
Arts Ambassador

Arsen Babajanyan is actively engaged in a social-cultural life and activities. He is a Chairman of the Association of Armenian Young Composers and Interpreters since 2010. Since 2016, Arsen Babajanyan is also co-founder and art director of ARTCONCEPT International Association, which realises art projects and programs all over the world. The art projects he is authoring missioning to setup a platform for cultural cooperation and to support the professional exchange between artists and cultural institutions. It aims at the establishment and the expansion of international collaboration networks and partnerships. Such an examples are Armenian Viola International Project or “Goodbye, Bird” chamber opera. He is also involved in different cross-cultural initiatives: Arsen Babajanyan with 6 other composers to take part in a big project, organized by the Old Gallery of Munich (Alte Pinakothek). His 11 compositions for the exhibited paintings of ancient masters accompanied the visitors along with exploring the paintings.

05
Social Activity

Though the main stream of Arsen Babajanyan's activity is composition, Arsen Babajanyan also has workouts in literary genre, where the scope of his activity was developed from scientific professional articles and theoretical works to literary compositions, prose and verse. Such as “Thoughts on Harmony” book publication dedicated to the life, music, musical- theoretical and philosophical views of composer, master and RA honored artist Professor Gagik Hovunts, a small book “The philosophy of Garegin Nzhdeh and its actuality” dedicated to Garegin Nzhdeh's works, which was awarded with the second prize by the Republican Party of Armenia, where the first prize was not awarded at all, etc..

Arsen Babajanyan is a laureate of “Irina Tigranova” nomination for the “Best Critical Article” in 2012, in 2009 he got 2nd prize for “The philosophy of Garegin Nzhdeh and its actuality” article in “Competition of Articles and Studies” by Republican Party of Armenia. In 2018, he has been awarded with Fridtjof Nansen Gold Commemorative Medal for the activities towards establishing humanitarian principles and condemnation of the Armenian Genocide.
